Treaty of Waitangi

Founding document of New Zealand is this Treay of Waitangi. It was signed on 6th Feb 1840. This is the agreement between Maori and English . 6th Feb is a public holiday.

The increasing british immigrants in New Zealand and they want to settle down extensibly. There were signs that the French were interested in annexing New Zealand. This treaty was prepard in few days. Lieutenant-Governor William Hobson had the task of securing British sovereignty over New Zealand. He relied on the advice and support of, among others, James Busby, the British Resident in New Zealand. Many chiefs supported the treat and assured that their status would be strengthened.

The Principles of this treaty are referred to in Acts of parliament. It is not a part of New Zealand domestic law.
